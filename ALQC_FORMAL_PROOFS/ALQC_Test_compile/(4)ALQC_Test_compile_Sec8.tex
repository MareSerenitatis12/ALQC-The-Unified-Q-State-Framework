\subsection{D-COMP: Dynamic Complexity Metric}\label{sec:8.10}

To quantify how complexity evolves as glyphs interact across dimensions, the ALQC uses a Dynamic Complexity (D-COMP) metric. While not explicitly named in the PDF, it is implicit in the quaternary resolution and M.A.S. calculations. We formalise it here to integrate the top-third content:

Let $(i,j)$ be a pair of dimensions with Q-state bias assignments $q_i, q_j \in \{0,1,2,3\}$. For a glyph with Q-vector $G_{i,j}=[Q_0,Q_1,Q_2,Q_3]$, define the local complexity as:

\[
C_{\mathrm{local}}(i, j) = |Q_{q_i} - Q_{q_j}| + |Q_2| + |Q_3|
\]

Here $|Q_{q_i} - Q_{q_j}|$ measures the differential tension between the preferred channels of the two dimensions, $|Q_2|$ quantifies the shadow residue present in the glyph, and $|Q_3|$ captures recursive capacity. The global D-COMP is then obtained by summing over all pairs:

\[
\mathrm{D\text{-}COMP}(G) = \sum_{i<j} C_{\mathrm{local}}(i, j)
\]

A high D-COMP value indicates a glyph with significant differential tension, shadow burden and recursion potential, requiring more energetic input from M.A.S. to stabilise. A low D-COMP value corresponds to a harmonious glyph that naturally fits within the Latin graphs and requires minimal correction.

This metric ties together the Q-vector assignments, D-state biases, frequency harmonics and the M.A.S. function, providing a single scalar measure of how demanding a glyph is on the lattice. In practice, the Dynamic Complexity guides how quickly recursion ($Q_3$) must be applied to avoid collapse and ensures the ALQC remains coherent.
