\subsection{Quantum Logic vs. ALQC}\label{appendixK.2}

Birkhoff and von Neumann observed that the join (logical "OR") of two atomic propositions about a quantum system can be "above" more atoms than either individually; consequently, the distributive law fails:
\[
r \land (p \lor q) 
eq (r \land p) \lor (r \land q)
\]
The orthomodular lattice of subspaces of Hilbert space replaces Boolean algebras as the structure of propositions. Within this landscape, the 	extbf{Ahnend Logical Q-State Core} provides a quaternary logic that 	extit{extends} quantum logic rather than competing with it.
